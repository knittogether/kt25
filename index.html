Welcome to the Knit Together 2025
The 1st European Spring School for Computational Linguists.

Amazing Research Arises Out Of Amazing Collaborations ...

Great research arises from amazing collaborations. However, establishing collaborations is challenging, and it depends on the context of the institution, the supervision, existing networks, and one’s personality. With KnitTogether25, we invite NLP researchers, particularly junior ones who work on Bias in NLP and Its Societal Impacts, to spend an exciting week in the Swiss mountains.
In a growing community dealing with a topic that is gaining increasing societal influence and relevance, we want to join forces and exchange ideas. What can we learn from each other?
In particular, but not limited to, we would like to explore: a) Which groups are under-/overrepresented or represented to their disadvantage? b) How can we incorporate such factors into modeling? c) How can we analyze them? We would like to hear the different perspectives of the participants: How do you relate your work to the topic? Using innovative and playful methods, we want to learn more about the work of our peers and thus get to know each other better scientifically
During a week full of hikes and other fun activities, the goal is to get to know each other without hurdles, draft ideas, and discuss research. We want to create a safe space where everyone can openly discuss their work and ideas without academic pressure.

Program

The week-long program, running from May 4th to May 10th, 2025, seamlessly blends structured research sessions, interactive workshops, and invited talks with ample opportunities for networking and social engagement. Each day balances academic and creative activities with meals and fun events, ensuring a mix of professional development and relaxation. The schedule is carefully designed to facilitate collaboration, innovation, and team building from check-in to check-out.

Venue

The Frauenfelderhaus is located 1160 meters above sea level on a ridge above Wildhaus opposite the Säntis. It offers space for up to 45 people and is equipped to a high standard. It is suitable as a camp house in summer and winter for school classes, clubs and other interested groups. It was donated to the Canton of Thurgau in 1957 by former students of the Kanti Frauenfeld secondary school fraternity.

Accomodation

The accommodation provides 45 sleeping places in a variety of room types—from basic bunk-style arrangements to more comfortable leader rooms with private facilities. It features a fully equipped kitchen, multiple common areas including a dining room and a ping-pong space, and outdoor amenities such as a terrace and a large playfield with a grill area. Additional conveniences include modern bathroom facilities, ample parking, and the option to rent a multimedia-equipped classroom.
